Privacy Policy

Corvus — Last updated: July 2026

In short: everything you create in Corvus — your species pages, notes, quick facts, and photos — stays on your device. Corvus has no user accounts and no servers of ours, and we do not collect your personal information. This policy explains the detail in plain language rather than legal jargon.

Who we are

Corvus is developed and operated by Matthew Morgan ("we", "us", or "our"), based in the United Kingdom. If you have any questions about this policy, you can contact us at mlmorgan4859@gmail.com.

Your guide stays on your device

Corvus is a field guide you write yourself. Everything you add — the species you choose, your notes, your quick facts, and any photos you take — is stored only in the app's own storage on your device. We do not have a server that holds a copy of your guide, and we cannot see what you write.

Because your content lives on your device, deleting the app also deletes that content. Corvus does not currently offer accounts, cloud sync, or backup, so please bear that in mind before removing the app.

What information we collect

We do not collect or store your personal information. The app does make a small number of requests to the internet to do its job, described below.

Species search queries
When you search for a species to add or identify a page, the text you type is sent to the iNaturalist API to find matching species and return their common and scientific names. No personal information is included in these requests.

Species photos from Wikipedia
If you choose to add a photo for a species from its Wikipedia article, the app requests that image from Wikimedia. This happens only when you ask for it. Photos you take or choose yourself are never uploaded — they stay on your device.

Standard technical information
Like any app that connects to the internet, when Corvus contacts iNaturalist or Wikimedia your device's IP address is visible to those services as a necessary part of delivering the response. We do not receive, log, or store this information ourselves.

Crash reports and diagnostics
If the app crashes, anonymised diagnostic data may be collected by Apple or Google through their standard platform frameworks. This helps identify and fix technical problems. We do not add any analytics or crash-reporting tools of our own.

We do not collect

  • Your name, email address, or any account information (no registration is required)
  • Your species pages, notes, or photos (these stay on your device and are never sent to us)
  • Your location
  • Contacts or communications
  • Browsing history or data from other apps
  • Any data for advertising, analytics, or profiling purposes

How we use information

The limited information described above is used solely to provide the app's core functions:

  • Search text is sent to iNaturalist to find a species and its names
  • A photo request is sent to Wikimedia only when you choose to use a Wikipedia image
  • Crash data helps fix technical issues and improve stability

We do not use any data for advertising, behavioural profiling, or any purpose beyond providing the app.

Third-party services

Corvus relies on the following third-party services, each of which has its own privacy policy:

  • iNaturalist — provides species search results. The text you search for is sent to their API. See iNaturalist's Privacy Policy.
  • Wikimedia / Wikipedia — provides species photos when you choose to use one. See the Wikimedia Foundation Privacy Policy.
  • Apple App Store / Google Play — distribute the app and may collect anonymised crash diagnostics through their platform frameworks. See Apple's and Google's respective privacy policies.

Data retention

Because Corvus does not operate accounts or store personal data on our servers, there is no personal data held by us to retain or delete. Your species pages, notes, quick facts, and photos are stored locally on your device and remain under your control. You can remove them at any time by deleting the individual pages or notes, or by deleting the app.

Your rights under UK GDPR

As a UK resident, you have the following rights regarding personal data:

  • Right to access — you can request a copy of any personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification — you can ask us to correct inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure — you can ask us to delete your data
  • Right to restrict processing — you can ask us to limit how we use your data
  • Right to object — you can object to certain types of processing
  • Right to data portability — you can ask for your data in a portable format

Because Corvus holds no personal data on our own servers, in practice these rights are exercised on your own device (your content is yours to edit or delete) or through the third-party services above. To make a request or ask a question, contact us at mlmorgan4859@gmail.com.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk if you believe your data has been handled improperly.

Children's privacy

Corvus is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information through the app, please contact us and we will take steps to address it.

Camera and photo permissions

Corvus asks for access to your camera and photo library only so that you can add a photo to a species page. Photos you take or choose are stored locally on your device and are never uploaded to us. You can grant or revoke these permissions at any time through your device settings; if you decline them, the rest of the app still works.

Changes to this policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the date at the top of this page. For significant changes, we will notify users within the app. Continued use of Corvus after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Contact
